MacArthur Lab of MGH and the Broad Institute of Harvard and MIT

The MacArthur Lab focuses on extracting useful information from human genome sequences. Some of the main focuses of the lab are accurate identification of sequence variants from massive data-sets, identifying LoF variants, using transcriptome sequencing approches to characterize the impact of DNA sequence variants on human gene function, and using genomic approaches to discover disease-causing mutations in severe disease patients.

Leiden Muscular Dystrophy Variant Database Cleanup

I have created a Python script and OpenRefine recipe to automate data extraction and cleanup from the Leiden muscular dystrophy gene variant databases. I have also converted variants given in HGVS notation (relative to cDNA transcripts) to genomic coordinates and validated the results via annotation in order to facilitate use in large-scale data analytics efforts.
