About Me

My name is Andrew Hill and I am currently a graduate student and NSF graduate research fellow in the UW Department of Genome Sciences in Seattle, WA. I work with Jay Shendure and Cole Trapnell on new technologies and computational methods for genomics.

Prior to coming to UW, I also worked as a research assistant and software developer in Daniel MacArthur's group in the Analytic and Translational Genetics Unit at Massachusetts General Hospital and the Broad Institute in Boston, MA.

Interests

Broadly, I am passionate about new technologies and methods in genomics. Specifically, I am currently very interested in problems in areas such as functional genomics, 3D genome architecture, cell fate decisions, and clinical applications of genomics.

While my current skill set is very much computational, I am expanding into experimental work as part of my projects with Jay and Cole.

Functional Genomics

I am interested in better tools and methods for more comprehensive and robust functional interpretation of genetic variation. This spans computational methods like CADD as well as functional assays like Saturation Mutagenesis.

Cell Fate Decisions

Single-cell assays such as single-cell RNA-seq and ATAC-seq are enabling dissection of complex dynamic processes like differentation. Currently, I am interested in both technological and computational advancements that exploit single-cell genomics assays to learn about cell fate decisions.

Clinical Sequencing

The dropping price of sequencing is moving to make clinical applications of next generation sequencing more widely viable. I am interested novel diagnostics and robust clinical interpretation of genetic variation.

Infrastructure

Scientific and clinical data are being generated in massive quantities. Effective infrastructure and algorithms that allow for rapid data visualizations and computation over large volumes of data are critical as the pace of data generation continues to accelerate.

Machine Learning

Machine learning algorithms have proven to be very powerful tools in various fields of research. I am interested in applications of machine learning within genomics and have contributed to projects like CADD in the Shendure Lab.

Software Tools

As an engineer, I am interested in developing robust tools and platforms that clinicians and researchers can apply within their own research.
